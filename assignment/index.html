The History of Library

After the conversion of us into a Municipal Corporation, Late Mr.P.H.Parmar, the retired Chief Administrator and City Engineer, and a voracious reader allowed us of establishing a Public Library in Surat. he was a great lover of books of different subjects including history. The Municipal was established during his tenure; but this library was open and free for the Municipal Corporation officers employees and for scholars pursuing higher studies.

Thus later it was a well-coming gesture of the Corporation to adopt public libraries for the citizens. But these reading rooms contained only newspapers and magazines. The readers had to sit there and read them. Various sorts of reading material published in the book form was not available for the readers to carry home. During this time other libraries like Govt. District Library and Private Libraries issued the readers, the books they like to read at home. So, the Corporation, with a idea of setting up a well-equipped library, so we make our library a full fledged library in 1981 in and keeped a seminar in Gandhi Smruti Bhavan. The Library provides the reading facilities to the public by charging nominal fees. People can read newspapers and magazines there and can carry books at home. This library had nearly 28000 books on various subjects and had subscribed around 100 journals and magazines.

Rules of the library

The Library is one of our most valuable assets. It provides the materials and the quiet environment needed for scholarly work. Please respect the library by following these rules:";

  1. It is forbidden to bring food into the Library.
  2. The only drink permitted in the Library is water in a container with a non-spill cap.
  3. Mobile phones must be switched to 'silent' mode.
  4. Conversations on mobile phones must be held outside of the Library.
  5. The rule of silence must be respected and antisocial behaviour will not be tolerated. Offenders will be required to leave, and repeat offenders will be banned indefinitely from using the Library.
  6. All books and periodicals must be returned to the shelves after use.
